Thais Roast Beef

Ingredients

  • leftover roast beef, cut into thin slices
  • 2 tbsp Thai fish sauce (nam pla)
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Thai fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 small garlic clove, very finely chopped
  • a couple of shakes of Tabasco or 1 tsp hot chilli sauce
  • 1-2 tsp caster or demerera sugar (to taste)
  • 1-2 tsp lime juice
  • 2 tbsp fresh mint, roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh basil, shredded
  • carrots, scraped into ribbons with a potato peeler
  • 1 red pepper, de-seeded and finely sliced into thin shreds
  • 1 red onion, halved and very thinly sliced
  • spring onions, sliced
  • cucumber, deseeded and cut into thin batons
  • mangetout, lightly steamed
  • cherry tomatoes, quartered
  • peanuts, crushed
  • lime wedges, to serve

Directions

  1. Combine the marinade ingredients. Since the beef is already cooked, it will not need to be left too long to marinade â€“ 15 minutes or so.
  2. Whisk together all the dressing ingredients, then refrigerate. (This dressing will last well for about 2 days.)
  3. When you're ready to cook the steaks, remove from the marinade and pat dry.
  4. Heat a ridged griddle pan (or heavy-bottomed frying pan) until smoking hot, then add the beef and cook until warmed through. Transfer the beef to a plate and set aside to make the rest of the salad.
  5. To serve, pile up the vegetables in a mound.
  6. Roughly chop the basil and mint and scatter over the vegetables.
  7. Top with slices of beef.
  8. Scatter over the peanuts.
  9. Serve with a wedge of fresh lime.
